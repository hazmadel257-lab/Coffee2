Courtado

  • Courtado

  • A cortado is a Spanish beverage consisting of espresso mixed with a roughly equal amount of warm milk to reduce the acidity, although the exact ratios have considerable regional variation.The milk in a cortado is steamed, but not frothy and "texturized" as in many Italian coffee drinks.The cortado is commonly served all over Spain. The word cortado is the past participle of the Spanish verb cortar (lit. 'to cut'), in the sense of to dilute, and can refer variously to either coffee or espresso drinks throughout Spanish- and Portuguese-speaking countries

  • Naming

  • The word cortado comes from the Spanish verb “cortar,” meaning “to cut.” The coffee was named so because of the way the milk “cuts” or reduces the high acidity of the espresso.

  • Preparation

  • To prepare a cortado, make two shots of espresso and then add an equal amount of warm, steamed milk (1:1 ratio). Typically served in a small cup, the milk reduces the acidity and bitterness of the espresso while creating a thin layer of foam on top.
  • Ingredients
  • Two shots of espresso, approximately 60-90 ml of milk (with an equal amount of espresso). Method: Prepare the espresso: Prepare two shots of espresso in a small cup. Heat the milk: Heat the milk in a saucepan over medium heat until it reaches a temperature of approximately (65°C) or slightly lower. At approximately 55-60°C, stir gently. You don't want it to become too frothy, just warm. Mix the ingredients: Pour the steamed milk over the espresso in a 1:1 ratio. Serve: Serve the cortado immediately in a small cup.
